## Today's Focus: SEC No-Action Letter Analysis & CFTC Threshold Research

### Completed Work

1. **SEC No-Action Letter Precedent Analysis**
   - **DoubleZero Foundation (September 29, 2025):** SEC granted no-action relief for 2Z tokens used to pay network participants in decentralized physical infrastructure network (DePIN). Key precedent: operational rewards for infrastructure participation do not trigger securities registration.
   - **Fuse Energy Token (2025):** SEC granted no-action relief for ENERGY token — utility tokens for energy trading/consumption without profit expectation.
   - **Helium (historical context):** Previous enforcement action demonstrates importance of clear no-action positioning before launch.
   
   **Critical Design Elements from Precedents:**
   - Token must be consumptive (burned/used, not held for appreciation)
   - No marketing of returns, yield, or profit participation
   - Decentralized governance with no controlling entity
   - Transparent, open-source functionality
   - Infrastructure/operational utility (not investment contract)

2. **CFTC Swap Dealer Registration Threshold Research**
   - **$8 billion AANA (Aggregate Average Notional Amount)** threshold triggers swap dealer registration
   - **Initial margin requirements** apply for uncleared swaps exceeding thresholds
   - **De minimis exemption** exists but requires complex calculation
   - **Crypto derivatives:** CFTC withdrew 2018 "enhanced" guidance — now folded into standard derivatives frameworks
   
   **2B2F Implications:**
   - Avoid synthetic exposure representations in token design
   - Avoid leveraged trading facilitation mechanics
   - Avoid margin-based token structures
   - Monitor AANA thresholds as GDP impact scales

3. **FSOC SIFI Designation Framework Analysis**
   - **Activities-based approach preferred** over firm-specific designation (2019 guidance reinstituted)
   - **"Very high bar"** for nonbank SIFI designation — no designations since MetLife (2014)
   - **180-day off-ramp** for risk mitigation before designation
   - **Cost-benefit analysis required** for any designation
   
   **Scaling Risk Trajectory:**
   - <1% GDP: Negligible SIFI risk
   - 1-5% GDP: Low risk — monitor, prepare
   - 5-20% GDP: Medium risk — engage FSOC proactively
   - 20-60% GDP: High risk — SIFI compliance design required
   - >60% GDP: Certain SIFI designation — full infrastructure required
