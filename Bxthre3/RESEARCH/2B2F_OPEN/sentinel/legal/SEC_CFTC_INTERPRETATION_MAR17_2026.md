## Executive Summary

On March 17, 2026, the SEC and CFTC issued a **joint interpretation** fundamentally reshaping U.S. digital asset regulation. This represents the most significant regulatory clarity since the DAO Report (2017).

**Core Finding:** Most crypto assets (BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP, etc.) are now explicitly classified as **digital commodities**, not securities.

**Strategic Impact for 2B2F:**
- ✓ Reduced regulatory risk profile
- ✓ Accelerated US market entry timeline
- ✓ Unlocked DeFi protocol infrastructure
- ✓ Enabled staking-as-service models
- ✓ Clarified airdrop distribution pathways

## The Five-Category Functional Taxonomy

| Category | Definition | Key Assets | 2B2F Application |
|----------|------------|------------|------------------|
| **Digital Commodities** | Value from programmatic operation, supply/demand | BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP, ADA, AVAX, DOT, LINK, DOGE | Core treasury assets, settlement instruments |
| **Digital Collectibles** | Artwork, gaming, cultural content | NFTs, game items, limited editions | Community rewards, cultural assets |
| **Digital Tools** | On-chain utilities | ENS domains, tickets, functional tokens | Infrastructure, governance |
| **Stablecoins** | Asset-referenced tokens | USDC, USDT, MiCA-compliant EUR | Treasury management, payment rails |
| **Digital Securities** | Tokenized stocks/bonds | t-bonds, equity tokens | RWA tokenization (Phase 3+) |

**Precedent-Setting:** Previous enforcement targets (XRP, SOL, ETH staking) are now explicitly **non-securities**.

## Critical Shift in Howey Application

### Old Framework (2017-2025)
**Primary Test:** Decentralization
- "Decentralized" network = not a security
- Development team exit = security status ends
- Focus on network topology

### New Framework (March 2026)
**Primary Test:** Issuer Representations
- A **non-security crypto asset** can become subject to investment contract when offered with **representations/promises of essential managerial efforts**
- Same asset can **cease** to be subject to investment contract when representations fulfilled, abandoned, or no longer relied upon
- Analysis turns on **reasonable purchaser expectations** shaped by issuer conduct

**Key Quote from Commission Interpretation:**
> "A non-security crypto asset may become subject to an investment contract when it is offered with representations or promises to undertake essential managerial efforts from which purchasers would reasonably expect to derive profits."

### 2B2F Communication Strategy Implications

| Activity | Pre-Mar 2026 Risk | Post-Mar 2026 Status | Guidance |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|----------|
| Roadmap promises | High risk | Medium risk | Avoid ROI promises |
| Yield projections | High risk | Managed risk | Protocol yields ≠ managed yields |
| Staking rewards | High risk | Low risk | Non-discretionary structure required |
| Team commitment statements | Medium risk | Continued risk | Managerial effort representations |

## Activities Now Explicitly Outside Securities Law

### Protocol Mining
- Public, permissionless network participation
- No issuer involvement
- **Status:** Non-security activity

### Protocol Staking
- No discretionary management
- No guaranteed yield
- Consensus participation only
- **Status:** Non-security activity

### Wrapped Tokens (1:1)
- Custodial receipt instrument
- No economic transformation
- **Status:** Non-security asset

### No-Consideration Airdrops
- No purchase required
- No service provided
- **Status:** Not an investment contract

### Mixed-Distribution Models — CRITICAL WARNING
When some assets in a class are sold and others airdropped:
> "If all tokens in a class are fungible, a purchaser could not know whether the tokens they received were the sold or airdropped tokens."

**2B2F Action:** Separate fungibility planning required for any mixed-distribution model.

## Boundary Conditions: When Securities Law MAY Apply

| Activity | Conditions | Risk Level |
|----------|------------|------------|
| Staking-as-a-service | Promised/guaranteed yield | Medium |
| Discretionary management | Active management of staked assets | High |
| Pooled exposure | Fractionalized or collective schemes | High |
| Synthetic derivatives | Exposure to underlying securities | High |
| ICO/Token sale with promises | Essential managerial efforts promised | High |
