## Tell us about your project

### Project Title
**AgentOS: A Living AI Workforce Orchestration System with Earned Autonomy via the Training Wheels Protocol**

---

### What problem are you working on?

AI agents are being deployed into enterprise workflows — scheduling, communications, operations, logistics — but no existing system provides a defensible mechanism for incrementally granting autonomy while preserving human accountability. The industry default is binary: either the AI acts autonomously (with all the liability that entails) or it's a chatbot with no real agency.

The result is that organizations either over-restrict AI systems (making them useless) or under-restrict them (making them dangerous and legally indefensible). There is no graduated, observable, auditable framework for saying "this AI has earned this level of trust through demonstrated pattern fidelity."

---

### How is your work different from what already exists?

Every major AI lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind) has safety policies, but these are internal and not externally auditable. No open-source or deployable framework exists that:
1. Operates in real production environments (not just benchmark safety evals)
2. Provides a graduated autonomy model (Mode 0–3) rather than binary allow/deny
3. Maintains a forensic audit trail that proves chain-of-custody for every AI-initiated action
4. Can be inspected by regulators, customers, or insurance underwriters

AgentOS is distinct from the BX3 Framework in that the BX3 Framework is a theoretical architecture for AI governance, while AgentOS is the live implementation of that architecture as an AI workforce orchestration platform. The BX3 Framework provides the formal reasoning; AgentOS provides the operational runtime.
