\begin.Abstract}
Autonomous multi-agent systems are increasingly deployed in high-stakes domains where algorithmic errors carry real-world consequences. Yet prevailing architectures treat human oversight as optional—a fallback activated only when the system signals failure—rather than as a structural obligation embedded within the agent hierarchy. This paper introduces the \textbf{Bailout Protocol}, a foundational component of the BX3 Framework, which mandates that any agent node encountering an unresolvable state within its defined boundaries must escalate accountability upward through the hierarchy until it reaches a designated human anchor. The Bailout Protocol is not a recovery mechanism; it is an accountability constraint. It operates on the principle that the boundary of an agent's authority is coextensive with its duty to recognize and transfer unresolved states, and that this transfer must occur before harm propagates rather than after it accumulates.

We formalize the protocol's preconditions, escalation triggers, and human-anchor response requirements. We distinguish escalatable uncertainty (states that exceed the node's epistemic or executive boundaries) from routine delegation (states that exceed its preference but fall within its authority), arguing that the former is the protocol's sole valid trigger. We further show that the Bailout Protocol enforces a directional guarantee: \emph{the system fails upward into human consciousness. It never fails downward into algorithmic chaos}. This directionality is architecturally enforced rather than probabilistically desired. We demonstrate the protocol's operation across three adversarial scenarios: cascading resource exhaustion, goal conflict between peer agents, and input distribution shift outside all trained distributions. In each case, the protocol constrains failure propagation and surfaces accountability at the human anchor before consequential action is taken autonomously. The Bailout Protocol provides a minimal, composable, and architecturally enforceable foundation for accountability in autonomous multi-agent systems.

\textbf{Keywords:} Bailout Protocol, human-in-the-loop, accountability escalation, autonomous systems, BX3 Framework
\end.Abstract}
