## TASK 1 — Today-Ready Grants

### 1. Survival and Flourishing Fund (SFF) — Speculation Grants

**What it is:**
The SFF is one of the largest funders in the AI safety / longtermist space. Its Speculation Grants program offers **expedited funding** for projects supporting long-term survival and flourishing of sentient life. In 2025, SFF allocated **$34.33M**, with ~86% going to AI safety organizations. Primarily funded by Jaan Tallinn.

**How to apply:**
Rolling application. Apply once and you're considered for both rapid Speculation Grants AND the next S-Process Grant Round.

- **Application form:** https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSd447GiP5BkyvHAKrybC-4jRcmIH2kHH2n86o4ruJb1TSfmKQ/viewform
- **Source:** https://www.effectivealtruism.org/opportunities/recrILIh6qqkvYqni

**Key details:**
- Decisions can come in as fast as **1 week** after applying (no fixed guarantee)
- Grants are awarded by a group of **~35 Speculation Grantors**, each with significant budgets
- Receiving a Speculation Grant **guarantees eligibility** for the next S-Process Grant Round
- Covers a broad scope: technical AI safety, governance, speculative work, field-building
- **No institutional affiliation required** — individual researchers and independent scholars apply directly

**Typical amounts:**
Varies widely by project and grantor discretion. The broader SFF ecosystem funds orgs at $150K–$500K+ levels; individual speculation grants are smaller ($5K–$50K range is typical but not guaranteed).

### 2. BlueDot Impact — Rapid Grants

**What it is:**
BlueDot Impact is a UK-based nonprofit that trains and funds AI safety researchers. Their Rapid Grants program is the most immediately accessible option on this list.

**Key details:**
- **Amount:** Up to **$10,000**
- **Decision time:** ~5 working days
- **Basis:** Rolling
- **What they fund:** Research projects, compute/API credits, events, meetups, travel, community chapters, fieldwork, project tooling
- **Application:** 5-minute application via Airtable — no lengthy proposal required

**Eligibility:**
BlueDot course participants, alumni, facilitators, and active community members. If you're in their network and doing excellent AI safety work, you're likely eligible.

**Good fit:**
> "You are doing something concrete — a research project, an event series, community building, or fieldwork — and a specific cost is the bottleneck."

** NOT a fit:**
General-purpose equipment, productivity subscriptions, or vague plans without evidence of work already underway. Stipends and living expenses are not the default (but you can apply and make the argument).

**How to apply:**

## TASK 2 — Science Fiction Foundation

**What it is:**
The Science Fiction Foundation (SFF) is a UK-based registered charity (No. 1041052) founded in 1971, based at the University of Liverpool. It holds **one of the largest SF archives in Western Europe** (40,000+ items), publishes the peer-reviewed academic journal *Foundation: The International Review of Science Fiction* (ISSN 0306-4964, twice yearly), and supports activities including the **Arthur C. Clarke Award** and the **George Hay Lecture**.

**What they fund:**
The SFF is **NOT a creative writing fund**. It funds:
- Academic research into science fiction (literary criticism, cultural studies, genre analysis)
- The SFF Collection (archive access and bursaries for researchers to visit)
- Educational outreach: the SFF Masterclass for sf criticism, Science for Fiction podcast, conferences

**Research bursaries available:**
The SFF offers bursaries for researchers to visit the Collection at University of Liverpool. There is also the **Maureen K. Speller Travel Fund** for researchers needing travel support.

**Who is eligible:**
Anyone — fans, students, independent scholars, academic researchers. Membership (voting rights + journal subscription) is available for ~£25–30/year.

**Stipends for writing:**
**No.** The SFF does not fund fiction writing, science education programs for the public, or general creative work. Its focus is scholarly research into SF as a cultural/literary phenomenon.

**Bottom line for BX3:**
Only relevant if BX3 is doing **academic research on science fiction as a genre or cultural form** — not applicable to AI governance or technical research.

**Source:** https://www.sf-foundation.org

## TASK 3 — Blue Dot Advocates

**Note:** "Blue Dot Advocates" does not appear to be a distinct organization. Searches consistently return **BlueDot Impact** (bluedot.org), which is likely what was intended.

**What BlueDot Impact is:**
BlueDot Impact is a UK-based nonprofit (Community Interest Company) focused on AI safety education, community building, and funding. They run free and paid courses on AI safety, career transition programs, and the Rapid Grants and Career Transition Grants described above.

**Is it a fiscal sponsor?**
**No.** BlueDot Impact is **not a fiscal sponsor**. It is a direct grantmaker — it gives its own money to researchers, not pass-through funding from other sources. Fiscal sponsorship would mean BlueDot holds your grant and handles legal/paperwork while you direct the work. BlueDot does not offer this service.

**How it helps independent researchers:**
1. **Rapid funding** — up to $10K in ~5 days for concrete AI safety work
2. **Career transition support** — funding + advising for people moving into AI safety full-time
3. **Community** — warm introductions, peer network, visibility to hiring managers at AI safety orgs
4. **Pathway into the field** — taking their courses (free or paid) makes you eligible for grants

**The key catch:**
You need to be part of the BlueDot community (course taker, alumni, active member) to access most grants. **Immediately actionable:** Take a BlueDot course (free), then apply for Rapid Grants.

**Source:** https://bluedot.org/about, https://bluedot.org/programs/rapid-grants
