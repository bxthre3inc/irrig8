## 1. USDA Forest Service — Wood Innovations Grant Program

**Program:** Wood Innovations Grant (WIG)
- **URL:** https://www.fs.usda.gov/science-technology/energy-forest-products/wood-innovation/grants
- **Amount:** $150K–$500K typical awards
- **Deadline:** Annual cycle (next: Fall 2026)
- **Match:** 50% required

**Relevance:** EXACT FIT for firewood marketplace
- Expands wood products and wood energy markets
- Supports "small-diameter timber" utilization
- Funds market creation infrastructure

**Subsidy Model Alignment:** 
- Medicare-style reimbursement = "market-expanding consumption subsidy"
- WIG supports "wood energy technical assistance" which includes consumer-facing programs
- Prior precedent: Maine's Statewide Wood Energy Assistance Team ([source](https://csfs.colostate.edu/science/colorado-woody-biomass/))

**Application Strategy:**
Frame as "market expansion for forest restoration byproducts" — Colorado State Forest Service completed 2025 statewide woody biomass analysis showing "abundant and underutilized" resource.

## 2. USDA — Community Wood Grant Program

**Program:** Community Wood Grant Program
- **Amount:** $50K–$200K
- **Purpose:** Ready-to-go local wood-for-heat/energy projects
- **Target:** Communities using wood for thermal energy

**Subsidy Model Alignment:**
Direct quote: "helps fund ready-to-go, local projects that use wood for heat and energy" — voucher-based delivery systems are qualifying activities.

## 3. USDA Rural Energy for America Program (REAP)

**Program:** REAP grants + loans
- **URL:** https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/energy-programs
- **Amount:** Up to $1M combined grant/loan
- **Target:** Rural small businesses + agricultural producers

**Subsidy Model Alignment:**
REAP funds "renewable energy systems" — a firewood marketplace that stabilizes winter heating costs qualifies as "energy efficiency improvement" at a community-system level.

## 7. Social Impact Bond / Pay-for-Success Model

**Oklahoma Precedent:** Oklahoma leads nationally in Pay-for-Success funding ([source](https://journalrecord.com/2025/11/26/oklahoma-impact-investing-pay-for-success/))

**Your Medicare Analogy Explained:**
- Government pays for outcomes (households stay warm)
- Marketplace operator keeps portion (like Medicare Advantage plans)
- Suppliers receive "reimbursement rate" ( below market, but volume-guaranteed)
- Users receive "free" wood (fully subsidized)

**Grant Opportunity:**
This is an innovation in service delivery — eligible for "Demonstration Grants" under LIHEAP or state Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP) innovation pilots.
