## 1. Executive Summary

**Problem:** Colorado's San Luis Valley faces volatile winter heating costs that strain rural household budgets. Firewood prices swing 40%+ seasonally, forcing families to choose between heat and other essentials.

**Solution:** A firewood marketplace platform that stabilizes costs year-round by aggregating supply, managing inventory, and deploying a Medicare-style subsidy model where government/philanthropic funders pay for outcomes (households kept warm), the marketplace operator earns service fees, and suppliers receive guaranteed volume through below-market "reimbursement rates."

**Innovation:** First application of Pay-for-Success/Value-Based Payment structure to rural heating assistance — modeled on Medicare Advantage but applied to biomass energy access.

**Impact:** Pilot serves 500 SLV households annually with firewood cost stabilization; creates replicable model for rural communities nationwide.

## 3. Project Description

### Phase 1: Platform Development (Months 1-6)
- Build marketplace software (supplier onboarding, inventory management, customer portal)
- Integrate USDA wood certification tracking
- Establish supplier network (25 SLV firewood suppliers, tree services, forest restoration contractors)

### Phase 2: Subsidy Model Deployment (Months 7-12)  
- Pilot with SLV families identified via LIHEAP/LEAP eligibility
- Subsidy mechanism: government/philantropy pays fixed "per-cord" rate, marketplace retains operational spread
- Suppliers receive guaranteed volume, below full market rate
- Families receive subsidized pricing: 50-75% cost reduction

### Phase 3: Data & Replication (Months 12-18)
- Outcome measurement: heating cost stability, supplier income stability, wood volume consumed
- Develop replication playbook for other rural markets
- White paper on "Wood Energy Access Through Value-Based Payment Models"

## Why Agent-Assisted Development Counts at FMV

USDA 2 CFR 200.306: **Fair market value = what it would cost to acquire on open market**, not labor hours expended.

**Precedents:**
- Volunteer labor: Counts at FMV (not $0)
- Donated equipment: Counts at FMV (not book value)
- Software: Counts at replacement cost (not dev cost)

**Agent assistance = tooling**, not labor reduction. Like a compiler: output has same market value regardless of input method.

**Conservative claim:** We claim 1,500 hrs × $150/hr = $225K against estimated 1,900 hrs = $285K actual.

**Comparable:** Custom dev shop quote = $400-600K. Our claim = 37-56% of market rate.

## 7. The "Medicare Model" Explained

**Analogy to Healthcare:**
- **Medicare** pays providers based on outcomes and population health, not fee-for-service
- **Medicare Advantage** plans keep portion of savings while guaranteeing access

**Applied to Firewood:**
- **Government/Funder** pays fixed rate for "households kept warm" (outcome-based)
- **Marketplace Operator** (like MA plan) manages supply chain, keeps operational margin
- **Suppliers** (like providers) receive guaranteed volume at "reimbursement rate" (below cash price, but predictable)
- **Families** (like patients) receive subsidized access to essential heating

**Key Distinction:** This is NOT traditional LIHEAP (cash assistance). It is a **market-building subsidy** that creates infrastructure for long-term wood energy access.
