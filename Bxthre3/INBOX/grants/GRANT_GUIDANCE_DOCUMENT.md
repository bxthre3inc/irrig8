# Grant Application Guidance Document
## Amber Grant (WomensNet) & IFundWomen — Winning Strategies

This document provides inline hints for the Irrig8 onboarding form to help applicants craft stronger grant applications for these two major women-owned business grant programs.

---

## 1. Amber Grant (WomensNet)

### What Makes a Winning Application

**Core Criteria:**
- Clear business vision rooted in passion or skill
- Demonstrated market demand (research or sales data)
- Compelling personal story and motivation
- Specific, realistic plan for using grant funds
- Track record of success or clear growth trajectory

**Winning Narrative Elements:**
- **Personal story**: Why did you start? What problem do you solve? Judges want to see the woman behind the business.
- **Passion + purpose**: Speak authentically — don't sound overly corporate. Judges value genuine dedication.
- **Market validation**: Show demand through sales data, customer base size, research, or waitlists.
- **Growth plan**: What specific bottlenecks will the $10K solve? (e.g., equipment, space, hiring)
- **Realistic budget**: Focus on growth investments, not survival costs. "I need working capital" is weak — "I need a commercial kitchen to increase production 5x" is strong.

**Ideal Application Length:** 600–1,000 words — detailed but concise.

### Common Reasons for Rejection

1. **Vague business description** — Can't articulate what you do or how you address a real market need
2. **No market validation** — No sales, no customer evidence, no research to prove demand
3. **Generic or corporate tone** — Sounds like a corporate application rather than a passionate entrepreneur
4. **Unclear use of funds** — "General business expenses" instead of specific growth investments
5. **Focusing on survival vs. growth** — Grant is for scaling, not keeping lights on
6. **Incomplete applications** — Missing required fields or rushing through questions
7. **No understanding of target market** — Can't identify who your customers are or how you compete

### Story Beats to Include

1. **The spark** — What moment or experience made you start this business?
2. **The problem** — What specific pain point do you solve for your customers?
3. **The solution** — How does your product/service fix that problem uniquely?
4. **The proof** — Show traction: revenue, customers, press, waitlist, repeat business
5. **The bottleneck** — What's limiting your growth right now? (capacity, equipment, reach)
6. **The ask** — Exactly how will you use the $10K to remove that bottleneck?
7. **The vision** — Where will this business be in 1 year, 3 years with this grant?

## 2. IFundWomen

### What Makes a Winning Application

**Core Criteria:**
- Strong alignment with specific grant partner criteria
- Clear social impact or innovation
- Compelling pitch video (if required)
- Well-defined business model and growth potential
- Professional online presence

**Winning Narrative Elements:**
- **Storytelling**: Craft a compelling, clear narrative about your business journey and goals.
- **Social impact**: Many IFundWomen grants prioritize businesses with community impact or mission-driven focus.
- **Unique value proposition**: What makes you different? Stand out from competitors.
- **Scalability**: Show how the business can grow and the grant will accelerate that.
- **Budget clarity**: Present a well-thought-out budget showing exactly how funds will be used.

**Key Distinction:** IFundWomen operates as a marketplace — they match applicants to specific sponsored grants. Your Universal Grant Application stays in their database, and you're notified when a grant matches your profile. This means your core application needs to be strong enough to match against ANY sponsor's criteria.

### Common Reasons for Rejection

1. **Incomplete applications** — Missing documents, incomplete business info, or poorly executed pitch videos
2. **Lack of alignment** — Business doesn't match specific grant criteria or sponsor priorities
3. **Weak pitch video** — Poor audio/video quality, unclear pitch, or failure to convey value proposition
4. **No clear impact** — Can't articulate how your business creates value for community/customers
5. **Missing financials** — No business plan, financial statements, or proof of registration
6. **Generic presentation** — No unique angle or differentiation from competitors
7. **No online presence** — Missing or unprofessional website/social media signals

### Story Beats to Include

1. **The "why now"** — Why is this the right time for your business?
2. **Your unique angle** — What specific gap do you fill that competitors don't?
3. **Impact story** — Who have you helped? Give a specific customer example if possible.
4. **The numbers** — Revenue, customers served, growth rate — whatever traction you have.
5. **The grant impact** — Exactly what will this funding enable that you can't do otherwise?
6. **Your ask** — Clear, specific dollar amount with a line-item plan.

## 3. Universal Tips (Both Programs)

### What Both Judges Look For

| Element | Why It Matters |
|---------|----------------|
| **Clarity** | Can you explain your business in 1–2 sentences? Judges read hundreds of applications. |
| **Passion** | Authentic enthusiasm beats polished corporate speak every time. |
| **Specificity** | Vague = rejected. "Grow my business" = weak. "Increase production capacity 5x" = strong. |
| **Traction** | Any sales, waitlist, press, or customer evidence proves market fit. |
| **Growth readiness** | Grants fund scaling, not survival. Show you're ready to grow. |
| **Complete applications** | Incomplete = automatic rejection. Every field matters. |

### Common Rejection Reasons (Both)

1. **Applying to wrong grants** — Don't apply to everything. Apply to grants that match your stage/industry.
2. **Incomplete documentation** — Missing business plan, financials, or required fields.
3. **Weak financial projections** — Unrealistic or unsupported numbers.
4. **Ignoring guidelines** — Not following eligibility, formatting, or word limits.
5. **No unique angle** — Generic businesses without clear differentiation.
6. **Focusing only on money** — Judges want to fund a vision, not just fill a funding gap.

### Inline Hints for Onboarding Form

Add these prompts or hints next to relevant form fields:

| Form Field | Hint Text |
|------------|-----------|
| **Business Description** | "Describe what you do in 1–2 sentences. How do you solve a specific problem? Judges see hundreds — make yours instantly clear." |
| **Why Did You Start?** | "Share your personal story. What moment or experience sparked this business? Authentic passion wins over corporate language." |
| **Market Validation** | "Show proof of demand: sales, waitlist, customer count, research, or press. Even early traction helps." |
| **Use of Funds** | "Be specific: 'Buy a commercial oven to increase output 5x' beats 'General business use.' Focus on growth, not survival." |
| **Target Market** | "Who is your customer? How big is the market? Show you understand who you're serving and how you reach them." |
| **Growth Plan** | "Where will this business be in 1 year? 3 years? Show you're ready to scale, not just stay afloat." |
| **Impact/Social Mission** | "What problem do you solve? For whom? How does your business make money AND create value for your community?" |
| **Financial Info** | "Include revenue, customer count, or any traction. Even small numbers show market validation." |
| **Pitch Video (IFW)** | "Keep it under 2 minutes. Clearly state: what you do, who you serve, the problem you solve, and how the grant helps." |

## 4. Quick Reference Checklist

### Before Submitting Any Grant Application

- [ ] **Business description** is clear and concise (30 seconds test)
- [ ] **Personal story** explains why you started and your motivation
- [ ] **Market demand** is proven with data (sales, waitlist, research)
- [ ] **Use of funds** is specific and tied to growth
- [ ] **Target market** is defined — who exactly do you serve?
- [ ] **Competition** is acknowledged — what makes you different?
- [ ] **Growth plan** shows you're ready to scale
- [ ] **Application is complete** — no missing fields
- [ ] **Format follows guidelines** — word counts, file types, etc.
- [ ] **Proofread** — no typos, errors, or generic language
