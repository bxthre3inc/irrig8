# RECORDING & DELIVERY GUIDE

## Single Recording Approach

**Record as ONE continuous voice memo.** Joby hears:
- 0:00-0:45: Complete hook with full ask
- 0:45: Natural pause with transition
- 0:50+: Deep dive if he keeps listening

**Why this works:**
- Respects his time — complete value in 45 seconds
- No "click here for part 2" friction
- Feels like a podcast episode structure (hook → deep dive)
- Natural conversational rhythm

## Delivery Cues

**PART A (0:00-0:45):**  
Confident but warm. This is the highlight reel. End with the risk/reward summary — don't hide the downside.

**TRANSITION (0:45-0:50):**  
Slight pause, shift in tone. "If you want the full picture..." — this is the opt-in moment. Then literal 5-second pause. Let the silence sit.

**PART B-I (0:50+):**  
Conversational storytelling pace. You're walking him through the business as if over coffee. Don't rush the numbers.

## Where Joby Might Stop

**At 0:45 (45 sec):**  
He's heard enough to decide if he's interested. Call to action delivered: "call me back or shoot me a text." He has everything he needs to engage.

**At 1:50 (after Part B):**  
Understands the structure, wants to know more about location/strategy.

**At 4:20 (after Part D):**  
Sees the vision, wants to understand financials/risk.

**At 5:05 (after Part E):**  
Appreciates the honesty, wants to hear risks and terms.

**All the way through:**  
Fully informed decision. Ready to discuss specifics.

## Follow-Up Text (Send with the voice memo)

## Technical Notes

**Recording environment:** Quiet room, no background noise  
**Device:** Phone voice memo is fine — authenticity over production  
**If you mess up:** Pause, restart from last section break. The structure allows for pickup points.

**Length check:**  
- Record Part A + Transition → verify 45-50 seconds  
- Record full thing → aim for 7-9 minutes  
- If under 7 min: probably rushing — slow down on numbers  
- If over 10 min: trim elaboration, tighten transitions
