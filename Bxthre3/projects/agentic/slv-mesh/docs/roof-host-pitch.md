## What We're Building
A free, off-grid text messaging network for the San Luis Valley.
When cell towers go down — from storms, fires, or emergencies — neighbors can still talk to each other.

---

## What We're Asking
A small weatherproof box on your roof or pergola, powered by your outlet.
That's it. No monthly payment. No contract. We handle the install and maintenance.

## The Ask (30-second version)
> "We're building a community safety network using small radio boxes on rooftops. It lets neighbors text each other for free, even if the internet and cell phones are down. We're asking for roof space and a regular outlet — we'll install it, maintain it, and it's completely free. Interested?"

## FAQ

**"Will this mess up my roof?"**
No. We use adhesive mounting or non-penetrating weights. We don't drill holes unless you're comfortable with it.

**"How much power does it use?"**
Less than a WiFi router. Typically 3-5 watts. About $1-2/year in electricity.

**"Does this affect my internet?"**
No. This is completely separate from your internet. It's a private radio network between neighbors.

**"What does it look like?"**
About the size of a paperback book. White or gray weatherproof box, small antenna. Designed to be discreet.

**"Can I message people on it?"**
Yes — anyone else on the network. You'll see neighbors, local businesses, emergency services that join.

**"Is it private?"**
Yes. Messages are encrypted. Nobody can read them except the sender and recipient.

**"What happens if I want it removed?"**
We uninstall it at no charge, any time.
