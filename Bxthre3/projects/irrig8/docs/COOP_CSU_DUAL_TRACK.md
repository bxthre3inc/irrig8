# PART II: MONTE VISTA CO-OP TRACK

## 2.1 The Co-op Value Proposition

**For the Co-op Board:**
- Member retention: Save farmers $25K/year → stronger co-op loyalty
- Competitive differentiation: Only co-op with precision ag offering
- Grant opportunities: Co-op as demonstration site for USDA/NRCS funding
- PR value: "Monte Vista Co-op leads valley water conservation"

**For Individual Farmers:**
- $0 cost (subsidized deployment)
- $25,200/year savings ($500/AF × 20% × 252 AF)
- Water Court compliance (Digital Water Ledger)
- No risk: If it doesn't work, uninstall at our cost

## 2.2 Co-op Outreach Sequence

### Week 1: Initial Contact

**Target:** Monte Vista Co-op General Manager
**Medium:** Phone call (Tuesday 10am — post-morning rush)

```
"Hi [GM Name], I'm Jeremy Beebe from Irrig8. We're a San Luis Valley company 
building a free precision irrigation system that could save your members 
$25,000 per year in groundwater fees.

We're looking for one co-op to partner with as our SLV demonstration site. 
The co-op gets national recognition, USDA grant opportunities, and member 
retention value.

Can I have 15 minutes this week to show you a 5-minute demo? I'm local — 
I can drive to Monte Vista any day that works."
```

**If no response in 48 hours:**
- Follow-up email with one-pager
- CC: Co-op board president (public info)
- Subject: "Following up: Free precision ag pilot for Monte Vista Co-op members"

### Week 2: Board Presentation

**Goal:** 20-minute board slot, approval for:
1. 10 farmer interview referrals
2. Demo field access (1-2 acres)
3. Co-op endorsement letter ("Partner in pilot program")

**Presentation Deck (12 slides):**

1. **The Crisis:** $500/AF fees, aquifer depletion, farmer exits
2. **The Solution:** Free precision irrigation ($0 cost to farmers)
3. **The Savings:** $25,200/year per pivot, ROI <6 months
4. **The Tech:** Sensor network, real-time data, VRI automation
5. **The Pilot:** 10 co-op farms, 1 season, our cost
6. **Co-op Benefits:** Member retention, grant opportunities, PR
7. **Validation:** CSU partnership (dual credibility)
8. **Timeline:** Deploy April, results by September
9. **Next Steps:** Farmer interviews, field access, endorsement
10. **Team:** Local founder, CSU advisors, engineering team
11. **Ask:** 20-minute board presentation this month
12. **Contact:** Jeremy Beebe, jeremy@irrig8.com, (XXX) XXX-XXXX

### Week 3-4: Farmer Interviews

**Format:** 30-minute interviews at co-op meeting space
**Compensation:** $50 gift card (co-op store credit)
**Target:** 10 farmers representing diverse operations

**Interview Script:**

```
"Hi [Name], the co-op recommended you as a knowledgeable grower. 
We're building a free soil monitoring system — no cost to you.

Quick questions:
1. What does your current irrigation decision process look like?
2. How much did you pay in groundwater fees last year?
3. If you could reduce that by 20%, what would that mean?
4. What would make you trust a new system like this?
5. Would you be willing to try it free for one season?

We're looking for 10 farmers to pilot this. If you're interested, 
we install in April, you use it all season, we remove it in October 
if you don't love it. Zero risk."
```

**Success Metric:** 8/10 farmers say yes to pilot

### Week 3: Research Collaboration Meeting

**Meeting Agenda (45 minutes):**

1. **Irrig8 Introduction (10 min):**
   - SLV native, personal stake in aquifer
   - Technical approach: 1m Kriging, 48" soil profiles
   - Status: 10 co-op farmers committed, seeking validation partner

2. **Research Proposal (15 min):**
   - Controlled trial: 3 treatments × 3 reps
   - Measurements: VWC accuracy, ET estimation, yield correlation
   - Duration: 1 growing season (April-October)

3. **Funding Discussion (10 min):**
   - USDA SBIR Phase I ($300K) — pending, needs academic partner
