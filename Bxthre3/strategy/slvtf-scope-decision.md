# SLVTF Scope Decision: Valley-Based vs. Valley-Focused

## Option A: Valley-Based Only (Headquarters in SLV)

**Pros:**
- Deep local economic impact — jobs, housing, services
- Easier to verify "real" commitment vs. grant-chasers
- Political capital with local officials (job creation stats)
- On-call for physical infrastructure issues
- Community trust — "they're one of us"
- Simpler legal/recruiting (one jurisdiction)
- Aligns with "modernizing rural areas" mission directly

**Cons:**
- Tiny talent pool — hard to find ML engineers in Monte Vista
- Slower to deploy — relocating teams takes months
- Higher risk — if startup fails, talent leaves, empty office
- Less deal flow — most climate tech founders are in Boulder/SF/Boston
- May force unnatural relocations for capital access
- Real estate/know-how gaps for urban-born founders

---

## Option B: Valley-Focused (Deploy Here, HQ Anywhere)

**Pros:**
- Massive deal flow — every ag-tech startup wants field deployment
- Faster time-to-subsidy — no relocation friction
- Founders keep their networks (VC, talent, exits)
- Can require physical presence 20%+ time (hybrid)
- Startups bring their own capital, just need infrastructure
- Easier to prove model with 5-10 pilots vs. 2-3 relocations
- Data/testbed value exists regardless of HQ zip code

**Cons:**
- Less direct job creation in Monte Vista
- Harder to capture full economic value (tax base, housing)
- Risk of "extractive" perception — use our land, take value elsewhere
- Local political support weaker without employment metrics
- May need residency minimums that are hard to enforce
- Equipment could leave if startup pivots away

---

## Recommendation: Hybrid "Valley-Anchored"

**Primary: Valley-Focused** — subsidize deployment regardless of HQ
**With teeth:**
- Require 6-month Monte Vista residency for at least 1 technical founder during subsidy period
- OR hire 1 local (valley resident) for integration/deployment role
- Equipment stays in valley even if startup fails (no shipping back)
- Annual "valley summit" — all startups present progress to local stakeholders

This gets you deal flow + local buy-in without forcing unnatural relocations.
