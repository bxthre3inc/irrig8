# SLVTF Hardware Model: Buyout vs. Retain/Rotate

## Option A: Startup Buyout Path (Lease-to-Own)

**Structure:**
- SLVTF owns Year 1 (fully subsidized)
- Startup pays 25% of depreciated value Year 2
- 50% Year 3, full ownership Year 4
- Or: Success fee triggers buyout at fair market value

**Pros:**
- Startup has skin in game after subsidy period
- Equipment stays productive (no orphaned hardware)
- SLVTF recovers capital for next cohort
- Aligns incentives — startup must generate revenue to keep gear
- Cleaner cap table — no lingering org relationships
- Easier for startup to raise next round (owned assets vs. borrowed)

**Cons:**
- Creates debt/overhang anxiety for struggling startups
- Valuation disputes — what's "fair market" for custom sensor rigs?
- Failed startups abandon equipment — SLVTF stuck with depreciated junk
- Buyout timing may not match startup funding cycles
- Legal complexity — liens, UCC filings, default handling
- Conflicts with non-profit status if seen as "lending"

---

## Option B: Retain & Rotate (SLVTF Asset Ownership Forever)

**Structure:**
- SLVTF owns all equipment permanently
- Startup has usage rights for 12-24 months (renewable)
- Post-subsidy: pay OPEX-only maintenance fee ($200-500/mo)
- If startup fails/pivots away: equipment redeployed to next cohort
- Failed startups return gear, no debt, no obligation

**Pros:**
- Non-pure charitable status clearer — ongoing service provision
- Equipment stays in valley ecosystem (circular economy)
- Failed founders aren't burdened with hardware debt
- SLVTF maintains fleet quality (can upgrade sensors centrally)
- Standardized hardware = easier maintenance, bulk purchasing
- Creates "valley infrastructure commons" — asset pool grows over time
- No valuation disputes — we own it, you rent it (cheaply)

**Cons:**
- SLVTF carries depreciation risk
- Startups never "own" their infra — investor concern?
- Requires SLVTF to handle maintenance/logistics ongoing
- Asset management complexity — tracking 50+ sensor suites across valley
- May feel "less committed" to startups vs. buyout path
- Insurance/liability on us permanently

---

## Hybrid Recommendation: "Graduated Commons"

**Years 0-2:** SLVTF owns, fully subsidized OPEX + CAPEX
**Year 3:** Startup chooses:
- A) Continue at OPEX-only rates ($300/mo rack + sensors)
- B) Buyout at depreciated book value (SLVTF sets price, no negotiation)
- C) Graduate — SLVTF redeploys gear, startup sources own infra

**If startup fails anytime:** Equipment returns to SLVTF, no penalty

**Key principle:** SLVTF is building the valley's shared sensing/compute infrastructure. Startups are the *first users* and *testers*, not the permanent owners. This aligns with Irrig8's "deterministic resource management" ethos — the substrate outlives any single application.
