# Organizational Structure: SLVTF as BX3 Subsidiary

**Confirmed Architecture:**
- **Bxthre3 Inc** (Parent) — For-profit entity
- **Irrig8** — Subsidiary product/service of Bxthre3 Inc
- **SLV Technology Foundation (SLVTF)** — Non-profit subsidiary of Bxthre3 Inc

---

## Structure: Non-Profit Subsidiary of For-Profit Parent

**Legal Form:** 501(c)(3) charitable organization
**Parent:** Bxthre3 Inc (owns controlling board seats or membership interest)
**Mission Alignment:** SLVTF's charter explicitly names Bxthre3/Irrig8 as "anchor tenant" and "technology partner"

---

## Key Compliance Requirements

### IRS Rules (Private Foundation vs. Public Charity)

**Risk:** If Bxthre3 controls SLVTF + SLVTF is primary funder of Irrig8 = self-dealing

**Mitigation:**
1. **Public charity status** (not private foundation) — requires broad public support
   - Target: 30%+ funding from government grants, small donors (<$5K), or other 501(c)(3)s
   - Avoid >20% funding from Bxthre3 or any single source

2. **Independent board majority** — 51%+ unrelated to Bxthre3
   - Valley stakeholders: farmers, educators, tribal representatives
   - Rotate 2/5 seats every 2 years

3. **Arm's-length transactions** with Irrig8
   - SLVTF pays Irrig8 fair market rate for:
     - Sensor hardware (if Irrig8 manufactures)
     - AgentOS platform usage
     - Integration services
   - Documented via competitive quotes or cost-plus pricing
   - No free services, no inflated prices

4. **Public benefit requirement**
   - SLVTF must serve broader class than just Irrig8 customers
   - Open application process, published criteria
   - Irrig8 gets no preferential access (though may be "first user" for beta testing)

## Strategic Synergies (Legitimate)

1. **Data Sharing**
   - SLVTF aggregated, anonymized data → contributes to Irrig8 models
   - Legal basis: SLVTF charter requires data sharing for "valley benefit"
   - Startups consent via application terms

2. **Technology Validation**
   - Irrig8 beta tests on SLVTF infrastructure (paid FMV)
   - Other startups validate alternatives, create competitive pressure

3. **Talent Pipeline**
   - SLVTF-funded startups hire valley residents
   - Irrig8 hires from that trained pool

4. **Political Cover**
   - SLVTF = "public benefit" voice in zoning, permitting
   - Bxthre3 = "business" voice in economic development
   - Dual lobbying more effective than either alone

## IRS Red Lines to Never Cross

| Prohibited | Permitted |
|-----------|-----------|
| Bxthre3 donates >50% of SLVTF funding | Bxthre3 donates <20%, others provide rest |
| SLVTF gives free Irrig8 services to startups | SLVTF pays Irrig8 FMV, startups pay SLVTF nothing |
| Bxthre3 board dominates (4/5 seats) | Independent valley stakeholders dominate (3/5) |
| SLVTF only serves Irrig8 customers | SLVTF serves any qualifying valley-focused startup |
| SLVTF assets transfer to Bxthre3 on dissolution | SLVTF assets transfer to other 501(c)(3) per charter |
